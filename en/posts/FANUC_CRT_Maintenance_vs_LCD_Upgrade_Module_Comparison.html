3.2 Failure Modes

CRT Common Failures:

  • Filament burnout (unexpected)
  • CCFL backlight degradation (gradual)
  • High-voltage section failure (dangerous)
  • Phosphor burn-in (permanent)
  • Focus drift (requires recalibration)

LCD Failure Modes:

  • Backlight LED gradual degradation (50,000+ hours)
  • Driver board failure (rare, <0.1%/year)
  • No high-voltage section (safe)
  • No phosphr burn-in (LED backlight)
  • No focus drift (fixed panel)

7. Decision Guide

7.1 When to Choose CRT Maintenance

Consider CRT maintenance ONLY if:

  • Display is <5 years old (CRTs typically last 10-15 years)
  • Repair cost is <30% of LCD upgrade cost
  • Display is not critical to production (spare available)
  • Budget is extremely tight (cannot afford upgrade)

7.2 When to Choose LCD Upgrade

Choose LCD upgrade if:

  • Display is 8+ years old (approaching end-of-life)
  • Display has failed 2+ times in past 2 years
  • Production downtime cost is high (CNC machine revenue >CNY 100/hour)
  • Image quality is critical (precision machining)
  • Energy savings are desired (green initiative)
  • Future-proofing is needed (CRT parts obsolescent)

7.3 Decision Flowchart

  1. Is the display 10+ years old? → Yes: Upgrade to LCD / No: Continue to step 2
  2. Has it failed 2+ times in past year? → Yes: Upgrade to LCD / No: Consider repair
  3. Are replacement CRT parts available and affordable? → No: Upgrade to LCD / Yes: Repair is option
  4. Is production downtime costly? → Yes: Upgrade to LCD (faster) / No: Either option
  5. Calculate 2-year TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) → LCD usually wins
