How to Retrofit FANUC CRT to LCD — Kongto Technology Case Study

2026-05-01 作者: 江图科技

Keywords: FANUC CRT to LCD retrofit, LCD conversion, CNC display upgrade, industrial display

Target Audience: CNC engineers, factory equipment managers, industrial automation integrators

1. Project Background

A precision machining factory in Shenzhen operates 15 FANUC 0i-MC CNC milling machines, all equipped with original 9-inch CRT displays manufactured between 2003-2008. After 15+ years of continuous operation, these CRT displays exhibited the following issues:

  • Screen flickering and brightness degradation (over 60% reduction from original)
  • Intermittent display failures causing unplanned production stoppages
  • Color distortion affecting operator ability to read tool position data
  • High-voltage circuit failures requiring increasingly frequent repairs (average 3 service calls per machine per year)

Annual maintenance costs per machine: approximately CNY 3,600 (parts + labor + production loss from downtime).

2. Solution Design

Kongto Technology proposed a comprehensive CRT-to-LCD retrofit solution:

ComponentSpecificationQuantity
FANUC A61L-0001-0093 LCD Display9-inch TFT-LCD, 800x600, 350 cd/m²15 units
Signal Adapter CableHONDA 20-pin to VGA15 units
Mounting KitStainless steel brackets + screws15 sets
Anti-Glare Filter9-inch optical grade15 units

Total project cost: CNY 52,500 (CNY 3,500/unit including installation)
Expected annual savings: CNY 54,000 (CNY 3,600/machine/year in reduced maintenance + downtime)

ROI period: Less than 12 months

3. Installation Process

A team of 2 technicians completed all 15 machines in 3 days (approximately 4-6 hours per machine):

Day 1: Machines 1-5

  1. Power off and verify complete discharge of CRT high-voltage circuit (wait 30 minutes after power-off)
  2. Remove original CRT display from mounting bracket (4 screws + HONDA 20-pin + 24V power)
  3. Install LCD display using original mounting holes, connect HONDA 20-pin adapter cable
  4. Power on and calibrate display settings in FANUC system parameters
  5. Run 2-hour burn-in test to verify stability

Day 2: Machines 6-10

Installation proceeded faster after the team optimized the workflow. Average time per machine reduced to 3.5 hours.

Day 3: Machines 11-15 + Final Testing

All 15 machines completed. Comprehensive system testing performed across all machines simultaneously.
