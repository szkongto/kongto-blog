Video Signal Converter Buying Guide — VGA-to-VIDEO vs HDMI-to-VGA

1. When Do You Need a Video Signal Converter?

Video signal converters are essential when connecting equipment with mismatched video interfaces. In industrial settings, the two most common scenarios are:

  • VGA to VIDEO (composite/S-Video): Connecting a modern industrial PC with VGA output to a legacy monitor or video distribution system that only accepts composite video input
  • HDMI to VGA: Connecting a modern device (laptop, media player, IPC with HDMI) to a legacy VGA monitor or industrial display

2. VGA-to-VIDEO Converters

2.1 Technical Overview

VGA-to-VIDEO converters transform analog RGBHV signals into composite video (CVBS) or S-Video (Y/C) output:

ParameterVGA InputComposite OutputS-Video Output
Signal TypeAnalog RGBHVComposite (CVBS)Y/C separated
ResolutionUp to 1920x1080480i/576i (NTSC/PAL)480i/576i
Color EncodingRGB componentNTSC or PALY/C (luma + chroma)
ConnectorHD15 (VGA)RCA (yellow)Mini-DIN 4-pin
Image QualityExcellentStandard (color bleeding, dot crawl)Good (reduced color artifacts)

2.2 Use Cases

  • Connecting a CNC controller VGA output to a factory-wide CCTV monitoring system
  • Recording CNC screen output on a standard video recorder for quality documentation
  • Displaying CNC screen content on a large TV in a training classroom

3. HDMI-to-VGA Converters

3.1 Technical Overview

HDMI-to-VGA converters transform digital HDMI signals into analog VGA output:

ParameterHDMI InputVGA Output
Signal TypeDigital (TMDS)Analog RGBHV
Max Resolution1920x1080@60Hz1920x1080@60Hz
AudioEmbedded in HDMISeparate 3.5mm output (pass-through)
ConnectorHDMI Type AHD15 (VGA)
PowerFrom HDMI port or USBN/A (output only)

3.2 Use Cases

  • Connecting a modern laptop with HDMI output to a FANUC CNC system's VGA monitor for programming or diagnostics
  • Using an HDMI-equipped IPC with legacy VGA industrial displays
  • Presentations using modern devices on older projectors

5. Recommendations

  • For CNC monitoring/recording applications: Choose VGA-to-VIDEO with S-Video output for better quality
  • For connecting modern devices to CNC monitors: Choose HDMI-to-VGA with audio extraction
  • For industrial environments: Always choose industrial-grade converters with wide temperature range (-20°C~+70°C)
