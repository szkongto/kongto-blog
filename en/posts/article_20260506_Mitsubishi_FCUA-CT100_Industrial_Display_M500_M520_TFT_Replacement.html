The Mitsubishi FCUA-CT100: Display Interface for M500/M520

The FCUA-CT100 is the core display interface for Mitsubishi M500/M520 CNC systems, responsible for signal transmission between the CNC controller and monitor. When the paired CRT monitor ages and fails, the FCUA-CT100 + MDT962B TFT combination provides a complete upgrade replacement solution — plug-and-play, thoroughly eliminating CRT aging and availability issues.

The FCUA-CT100 performs the critical function of converting CNC controller signals into display driver signals. When paired with our MDT962B TFT LCD replacement screen, it forms a complete modern display solution that eliminates all CRT-related issues.

Common CRT Failure Symptoms

After years of use, the original CRT monitors for M500/M520 systems commonly exhibit these issues. When you notice any of these symptoms, it's time to upgrade to TFT LCD.

⚡

Electron gun aging

🔥

High-voltage pack failure

⬜

CRT tube burnout
Complete display failure, equipment downtime — production stops

📦

Spare parts scarcity
Original CRT discontinued for years, inflated prices for unreliable used units

👁️

Operator eye strain
Flickering and unclear display causes fatigue during long operations

💸

Rising maintenance costs
Professional CRT repair is expensive and provides only short-term fixes

The FCUA-CT100 interface board itself rarely fails. The problem is almost always the CRT monitor it drives. The solution: keep the FCUA-CT100 and replace only the CRT with our MDT962B TFT LCD.

Step-by-Step Installation Guide

Installing the FCUA-CT100 + MDT962B TFT LCD upgrade is straightforward and can be completed in about 1 hour. No specialized tools or technical expertise required.

1️⃣

Power Down & Safety Check

Confirm equipment is completely powered off. Wait 5 minutes for high-voltage components to discharge. Verify no power indicator lights are illuminated.

2️⃣

Remove Original CRT Monitor

Unplug the CRT power and signal cables from the back of the monitor. Unscrew the mounting bolts (typically 4 screws). Carefully remove the CRT unit.

3️⃣

Install TFT LCD Screen

Position the new MDT962B TFT LCD into the same mounting holes. The hole pattern is 100% identical — no drilling or modifications needed. Secure with the original screws.

4️⃣

Connect FCUA-CT100 Signal Cable

Plug the TFT LCD signal cable into the FCUA-CT100 interface board. The connector is keyed — it only fits one way. No adapters or rewiring needed.

5️⃣

Power On & Test

Restore power to the machine. The TFT LCD will power on instantly — no warm-up time. Verify display clarity and run a test program to confirm everything works.

✅

Complete!

Entire process requires no programming or parameter changes. The CNC system automatically recognizes the new display. Your machine is ready for production.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need to replace the FCUA-CT100 board too?

A: No! The FCUA-CT100 interface board rarely fails. Only the CRT monitor needs replacement. Our TFT LCD connects directly to the existing FCUA-CT100 board — plug-and-play.

Q: Will this work if my FCUA-CT100 board is old?

A: Yes. The FCUA-CT100 board's function is signal conversion, which doesn't degrade over time like CRT displays. As long as your FCUA-CT100 is functioning, it will work perfectly with our TFT LCD.

Q: What if I don't have the original FCUA-CT100 board?

A: We also supply replacement FCUA-CT100 interface boards. Contact us for pricing and availability. However, most customers only need the TFT LCD upgrade kit.

Q: Is the installation really plug-and-play?

A: Absolutely. The TFT LCD uses the same signal connector as the original CRT. Simply unplug the CRT, plug in the TFT, mount it with the same screws — done. No rewiring, no adapters, no CNC parameter changes.

Q: How long does the TFT LCD last?

A: Our TFT LCD panels are rated for 50,000+ hours MTBF. At 8 hours/day operation, that's over 17 years of maintenance-free service. Compare that to CRTs which typically last 15,000 hours.

Q: Do you provide technical support?

A: Yes! We provide free technical support for all our products. Our engineers are available via phone, email, WhatsApp, or WeChat to assist with installation questions or troubleshooting.
