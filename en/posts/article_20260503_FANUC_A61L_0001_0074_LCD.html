FANUC A61L-0001-0074 14-inch Color CRT to LCD Retrofit – Complete Technical Solution & Installation Guide

Published: 2026-05-03 Updated: 2026-05-16 By: Kongto Technology Technical Department

1. Overview

The FANUC A61L-0001-0074 is a widely deployed 14-inch color CRT monitor in legacy FANUC 0i, 16i, 18i series CNC systems and Nanjing CNC controllers. After years of industrial operation, original CRT monitors commonly exhibit brightness decay, color distortion, screen flicker, focus blur, and eventual functional failure. This document provides a complete technical retrofit solution to replace obsolete CRT units with an industrial-grade TFT LCD module, supporting direct plug-and-play replacement without CNC system modification.

2.2 Common Aging Failures

  • Progressive Brightness Reduction: CRT phosphor and cathode aging lead to continuous brightness decay, resulting in poor daytime visibility and operator eye strain.
  • Color Cast and Permanent Color Shift: RGB gun aging imbalance causes color temperature drift (red appears orange, blue appears purple), affecting G-code highlighting and alarm message recognition.
  • Intermittent Screen Flicker and Geometric Distortion: Deflection coil aging and focus circuit degradation cause image jitter, pincushion distortion, and trapezoidal deformation, making machining program display unclear.
  • High-Voltage Insulation Degradation: Prolonged high-voltage operation (10-15kV) causes insulation aging, potentially leading to high-voltage leakage or arcing, posing fire hazard and equipment damage risk.
  • Complete Black Screen Failure: Cathode ray tube filament burnout or high-voltage package failure renders the display completely inoperable, causing unplanned CNC system downtime.
  • Obsolete Component Supply: Original CRT components are discontinued; replacement parts are rare and expensive, with extended maintenance lead times that disrupt production schedules.

5. Standard Installation Procedure (30-40 Minutes)

The KTV148 LCD module is designed for direct mechanical and electrical compatibility with the original A61L-0001-0074 CRT. No CNC system parameter changes are required. The entire retrofit can be completed in 30-40 minutes.

5.1 Safety Preparation

  1. Shut down and disconnect the main power supply of the CNC system. Ensure complete power isolation.
  2. Allow 8-10 minutes for internal high-voltage capacitors to fully discharge. Verify with a multimeter if possible.
  3. Use proper ESD protection (anti-static wrist strap) to prevent electrostatic damage to the LCD module's electronic components.
  4. Take photographs of the existing wiring and CRT orientation before disconnection for reference during reinstallation.

5.2 Original CRT Removal

  1. Disconnect the HONDA 20-pin signal cable from the rear of the CRT. Press the release latch and pull firmly but gently.
  2. Disconnect the DC 24V power cable from the CRT power connector.
  3. Remove 4-6 mounting screws (typically M4 or M5) that secure the CRT bezel to the control cabinet.
  4. Carefully extract the CRT assembly from the cabinet. CRT units are heavy (8-12kg) and fragile; use proper lifting technique and avoid shaking.

5.3 LCD Module Installation

  1. Unpack the KTV148 LCD module. Inspect for shipping damage. Verify the model label matches A61L-0001-0074 compatibility.
  2. Position the LCD module into the original CRT mounting space. The KTV148 is designed with identical outer dimensions (194×146mm mounting hole pattern).
  3. Align the mounting holes. The module is keyed (cannot be installed incorrectly). Gently push until the module sits flush against the cabinet panel.
  4. Secure with the original 4-6 mounting screws. Do not overtighten; snug fit is sufficient. Overtightening can distort the front panel and affect display uniformity.

5.4 Cable Connection

  1. Connect the HONDA 20-pin signal cable to the LCD module's signal interface. The connector is keyed; if it doesn't slide in easily, do not force it. Reverse and retry.
  2. Connect the original DC 24V power cable to the LCD module's power connector. The KTV148 operates directly from the machine's existing 24V power supply; no additional power adapter or voltage converter is needed.
  3. Ensure all connectors are fully seated and the locking latches (if present) are engaged. Gently tug on each connector to verify secure connection.

5.5 Power-On Test and Calibration

  1. Restore main power to the CNC system. Power on the system using the standard procedure.
  2. The KTV148 LCD module will display the CNC boot screen automatically. No manual switching or mode selection is required.
  3. Verify display clarity: text characters should be sharp and well-defined at all screen positions.
  4. Verify color accuracy: G-code syntax highlighting, alarm messages (typically red or yellow), and menu colors should display correctly.
  5. Adjust brightness and contrast via the OSD (On-Screen Display) menu if needed. The OSD is accessed via buttons on the front or side panel of the LCD module.
  6. Adjust screen position and phase via OSD if the image is shifted or has residual flicker. The auto-adjust function typically resolves positioning issues in <10 seconds.
  7. Run the CNC system for at least 30 minutes to verify thermal stability. The LCD module should operate without overheating or image degradation.

Important Notes:

  • The entire retrofit process requires no modification to CNC system parameters. The KTV148 is electrically and mechanically transparent to the CNC controller.
  • Do not attempt to open the CRT housing. CRT units contain high-voltage components (10-15kV) that retain dangerous charge for hours after power disconnection.
  • If the LCD module fails to display after installation, first verify the HONDA 20-pin connector is fully seated. Then check the DC 24V power supply voltage at the connector (should be 21-27V DC).

  • Extended Service Life, Reduced Downtime: With a rated lifespan of 50,000+ hours (vs. 10,000-15,000 hours for CRT), the LCD module eliminates unplanned display failures. This is particularly critical for 24/7 production environments where downtime costs thousands of dollars per hour.

  • Zero High-Voltage Hazard: Unlike CRT displays that operate at 10-15kV, the LCD module uses only low-voltage DC 24V power. This eliminates the risk of high-voltage leakage, arcing, or electrical shock during maintenance.
  • OSD Menu for Fine Adjustment: The built-in OSD (On-Screen Display) allows adjustment of brightness, contrast, screen position, phase, clock, and color temperature. The menu is navigated via front-panel buttons and can be locked to prevent accidental changes.

7. Operation and Maintenance Guidelines

7.1 Periodic Inspection (Every 6 Months)

  • Inspect the HONDA 20-pin signal cable connector for looseness, oxidation, or bent pins. Reseat the connector if necessary.
  • Check the DC 24V power cable and connector for tight connection. Measure voltage at the connector (should be 21-27V DC under load).
  • Inspect the LCD module's front panel for dust, oil splashes, or chemical residue. Clean with a soft, lint-free cloth lightly dampened with isopropyl alcohol (70% or higher). Do not use ammonia-based cleaners (e.g., Windex), as they can damage the anti-glare coating.
  • Verify that the OSD menu is still accessible and that display parameters (brightness, contrast, position) have not drifted.

7.2 Long-Term Storage and Backup

  • If the original CRT is still functional, store it as a temporary backup. Keep it in a dry, temperature-controlled environment (15-25°C, <60% RH).
  • Spare KTV148 modules can be stored for 5+ years without degradation. Rotate stock if multiple spares are kept.
