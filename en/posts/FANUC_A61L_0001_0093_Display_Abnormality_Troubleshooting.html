1. Overview of A61L-0001-0093

3. Diagnostic Steps

Step 1: Visual Inspection

  • Check power indicator LED (should be green or orange)
  • Inspect HONDA 20-pin connector for loose or damaged pins
  • Look for physical damage (cracks, burns, swelling capacitors)
  • Smell for burnt components

Step 2: Power Supply Test

  • Measure input voltage at HONDA connector pin 1&2 (should be 24V DC ±10%)
  • Check fuse continuity (located on power board)
  • Test power supply output with multimeter
  • Verify grounding connection

Step 3: Signal Test

  • Check HONDA 20-pin connector pinout (reference FANUC manual)
  • Measure signal voltage levels (RGB: 0.7V p-p, Sync: TTL level)
  • Test with known good signal source if possible
  • Verify horizontal/vertical sync signals

Step 4: CRT Specific Tests

  • Check CRT filament continuity (should be 0.5-2Ω)
  • Measure high-voltage output (should be 15-25kV)
  • Listen for high-pitched whine (indicates high-voltage section working)
  • Check for flicker (indicates CRT aging)

4. Power Supply Issues

4.1 Fuse Blown

Symptoms: No power indicator, completely dead display

Solution:

  1. Disconnect power and discharge capacitors (wait 5+ minutes)
  2. Locate and remove blown fuse (usually 2A/250V slow-blow)
  3. Check for short circuits before replacing (prevents immediate re-blow)
  4. Install new fuse and test

4.2 Power Board Failure

Symptoms: Fuse intact but no power output

Solution: Replace power board or repair damaged components (capacitors, transistors, diodes). Note: CRT high-voltage section is dangerous! Leave to qualified technicians.

5. Signal Problems

5.1 Loose or Damaged HONDA 20-pin Connector

Symptoms: Intermittent display, flickering, "No Signal" message

Solution:

  1. Power off CNC system
  2. Re-seat HONDA 20-pin connector (ensure all pins engaged)
  3. Inspect cable for broken wires or damaged pins
  4. Replace cable if damaged
  5. Apply contact cleaner to connector pins

5.2 EMI Interference

Symptoms: Ghosting, distorted image, random flickering

Solution:

  1. Check grounding of display and CNC system (resistance < 1Ω)
  2. Add ferrite cores to signal cables
  3. Re-route signal cables away from power lines (min 30cm separation)
  4. Install EMI filters if necessary

6. CRT Failure Symptoms

6.1 CRT Aging (Most Common)

Symptoms: Dim display, takes 5+ minutes to warm up, flickering, colors shifted

Causes:

  • CRT filament aging (emission capability decreased)
  • Phosphor layer aging (brightness reduced by 50%+)
  • Electron gun aging (focus deterioration)

Solution: CRT replacement is not economical. Recommended to upgrade to LCD. LCD upgrade kit costs ~CNY 2,500-3,500, while CRT replacement part is rare and expensive (if available at all).

6.2 High-Voltage Section Failure

Symptoms: No high-voltage output, screen completely dark, no filament glow

Solution: Dangerous to repair! High-voltage section operates at 15-25kV. Recommend replacing entire display or upgrading to LCD.
