3. Retrofit Implementation Steps

Step 1: Pre-installation Assessment

Confirm the original display's signal type (CGA 15kHz or EGA 25kHz) and connector pinout. Document the existing cable routing for reference.

Step 2: Power Off & ESD Protection

Shut down the machine tool main power. Wear an anti-static wrist strap and connect to ground. Verify all power indicator lights are off.

Step 3: Install the Signal Converter

Connect the CGA/EGA output cable from the CNC controller to the converter input. Connect the converter's RGBHV (VGA) output to the new display. Secure all connections with cable ties.

Step 4: Power Connection

Connect the converter's 24V DC power supply. Verify polarity before powering on. The converter draws less than 2W.

Step 5: Signal Verification

Power on the CNC system and verify the display output. Check resolution settings in the FANUC system parameters. Adjust if necessary.

Step 6: Secure Installation

Mount the converter in a vibration-free location inside the electrical cabinet. Ensure adequate ventilation. Label all connections for future maintenance.

4. Performance Evaluation & Maintenance

After retrofit, verify the following performance indicators:

  • Display stability: no flicker, no signal loss during 8-hour continuous operation
  • Image quality: clear text, accurate colors, no ghosting
  • Temperature: converter surface temperature below 50°C under load
  • EMI compliance: no interference with other CNC subsystems

Maintenance Schedule: Inspect connections every 6 months. Clean dust from converter ventilation openings annually. Expected converter service life: 5+ years.
